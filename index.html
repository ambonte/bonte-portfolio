7 Habits of successful developers

Success is not an overnight trip but a long journey of hard work and dedication. To make your mark in the software development industry, you have to be passionate about coding and should be ready to go to any extent to improve yourself. Even if it requires you to change the very core of your habits, you should be prepared for that. This is what every successful developer does. Here, let us discuss seven most impactful habits of successful developers that you can follow to reach the top of your career. 1. Writing clean code The first good habit that has the potential to make you a successful developer is to write a clean code. You can follow any coding standard you like but make sure your code is human readable. Technology is changing very rapidly and there may be instances when your code needs improvements or tweaks. So, make your code maintainable enough that others can also make changes to your code whenever needed in the future. 2. Learning continuously The phenomenon of ‘the survival of the fittest’ works in the software ecosystem too. To survive in this competitive world, you need to continuously improve yourself. Employers today want their workforce to be up-to-date with industry standards and software technologies. Thus, to keep pace with the advancements, you can join skill enhancement programs, online trainings, read tutorials, watch videos, etc. Upskilling yourself will definitely be a turning point in your career as a software developer. 3. Discipline and Persistence Discipline and persistence are other success-oriented habits that every developer should adapt to. Being disciplined helps you finish your task-in-hand on time. Persistence, on the other hand, is another virtue of successful developers that strengthen them to beat all odds and complete their work before the deadline hits no matter what the hurdles they are facing. 4. Knowing their forte Not everyone is made for doing everything. You may be good at developing UI and UX but may not be an expert at developing the back-end of an application. Successful software developers know their forte and work around that to succeed in whatever they do. 5. Open for new things To meet a client’s expectation, a developer may be required to go an extra mile in researching and exploring the every aspect of technology. It is said that “Impossible” is not a word in a developer’s dictionary once the requirement document is closed. Successful developers follow this rule by their heart and that is the quality which eventually makes them successful. 6. Paying attention to details Any successful developer can tell you that to write a perfect code or to complete a project successfully, you need to pay attention to the details of the client requirement and work accordingly. You would not want to work on a piece of code again and again because of a small semicolon that you forgot to add while terminating a line of code. Thus, keep your eyes open and attend every small detail of the code and the project as a whole to be in the category of successful developers. 7. Having end product in mind all the time You cannot hit a target you cannot see. It is imperative for you to set clear goals of where you are headed toward and what you really want at the end of your project. If you start developing a project keeping this mantra in your mind, there is no way you won’t succeed as a developer.

Do Something You Love And You’ll Never Work A Day In Your Life

I remember reading this quote a few times and never thinking much of it. Growing up I never had any idea of what I wanted to do. This continued as I got older and graduated from university. I still had no clue about which path I wanted to take. I ended up travelling and doing a lot of work in the construction industry. I thought this may be something that I would enjoy doing. I get a lot of satisfaction from building things, but working on construction is truly hard work. Could I do that for the rest of my life? It was a question I couldn’t answer. As luck would have it, I decided to start a travel blog after being unable to find information on somewhere I was going. While it was tough going at first, I was able to slowly build up some momentum with the blog after a while. It was during these early periods and later on when I came home from working in the office that I realised how much I enjoyed working on the blog. I got an immense amount of satisfaction from writing a post and seeing it perform well on Google. It was at this moment that I realised I didn’t consider the blog work. I thoroughly enjoyed sitting down and doing hard work, even though it didn’t feel like hard work. I realised the value of the above quote in these moments. Not all of us can do what we enjoy for a living. However, I think the main reason for this is that a lot of us don’t know what we enjoy doing when it comes to working. A large reason for this is that we neglect to do hard work when it matters the most. If you start a passion project but give up halfway through because you aren’t seeing results, you will never know what could have been. The pain of regret will always be there.
